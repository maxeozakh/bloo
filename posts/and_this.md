The moon arrived late, wearing a velvet crown of borrowed stars and speaking in footnotes. In the market of glass oranges, a knight traded his shadow for a map that only worked while forgotten. The towers leaned like tired questions. 

Somewhere a dragon, retired and theoretical, warmed a teacup with one polite spark. By dawn the city had edited itself again, leaving only a red door in the meadow, and a rumor that history was just a costume the wind kept revising.
